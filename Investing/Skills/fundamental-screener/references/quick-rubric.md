# Condensed Scoring Rubric (0–100)

Same six pillars and weights as the full fundamental-analysis rubric —
scores from a screen should match a deep dive within a few points. Score
each pillar at the band midpoint unless evidence clearly pushes higher or
lower within it. Compare margins/returns against the company's industry,
not the whole market. Missing data → bottom of the middle band, never
zero, never benefit of the doubt.

## 1. Profitability & quality — 25 pts

| Band | Points |
|---|---|
| ROIC >20%, strong margins vs. peers, FCF ≈ net income | 20–25 |
| ROIC 12–20%, solid margins, decent FCF conversion | 14–19 |
| ROIC 7–12%, or good level but erratic; FCF lags earnings | 8–13 |
| Barely profitable or margins compressing | 3–7 |
| Unprofitable / negative FCF with no clear path | 0–2 |

## 2. Growth — 20 pts

| Band | Points |
|---|---|
| Revenue CAGR >12% and steady; EPS growing faster | 16–20 |
| Revenue 6–12%, per-share growth positive | 11–15 |
| Revenue 2–6%, flattish EPS | 6–10 |
| Flat revenue or boom-bust cyclicality | 2–5 |
| Shrinking | 0–1 |

## 3. Financial health — 15 pts

| Band | Points |
|---|---|
| Net cash, or net debt/EBITDA <1x; coverage >10x | 13–15 |
| Net debt/EBITDA 1–2x; coverage 4–10x | 9–12 |
| 2–3x; coverage adequate | 5–8 |
| >3x or rising leverage funding payouts | 1–4 |
| Solvency in question | 0 |

## 4. Competitive moat — 15 pts

| Band | Points |
|---|---|
| Dominant + 2 durable moat sources, visible in 5-yr numbers | 12–15 |
| Clear leader, one strong moat source | 8–11 |
| Decent position, replicable; execution-driven | 4–7 |
| Commodity price-taker or losing share | 0–3 |

## 5. Valuation — 15 pts

| Band | Points |
|---|---|
| Below own history/peers without a quality reason; FCF yield >5% or PEG <1.5 | 12–15 |
| In line with history; FCF yield 3–5% or PEG 1.5–2.5 | 8–11 |
| 20–50% above norms; PEG 2.5–4 | 4–7 |
| Extreme multiple, story-priced, or unprofitable | 0–3 |

## 6. Management & shareholders — 10 pts

| Band | Points |
|---|---|
| Shrinking share count, sensible capital allocation, covered growing dividend | 8–10 |
| Mild dilution (<2%/yr), mixed record | 5–7 |
| Notable dilution or questionable M&A | 2–4 |
| Serial diluter, dividend funded by debt, accounting issues | 0–1 |

## Red-flag caps (apply after summing; state in the takeaway)

| Red flag | Cap |
|---|---|
| Going-concern doubt / needs cash within 12 months to operate | 25 |
| Restatement, credible fraud allegations, auditor resignation (2 yrs) | 30 |
| Persistently negative FCF AND net debt/EBITDA >3x | 35 |
| One customer/product >40% of revenue with expiry risk | 55 |
| Core product in structural decline | 55 |
| Dividend payout >100% of FCF for 2+ years | 60 |

## Buy-below price (for scores ≥ 50)

The price at which the stock becomes good value per this rubric. Compute
it the same way every time so prices are comparable across tickers:

1. **Normalized earnings power per share.** Use TTM EPS or FCF/share,
   whichever better reflects real cash economics. For cyclicals, use
   the mid-cycle estimate (same one used to score the valuation pillar).
2. **Fair multiple.** Start from the company's own 5-yr median P/E (or
   P/FCF), then sanity-check against quality: a 15–20x band for steady
   quality, 20–25x only for genuinely superior businesses (moat 12+,
   ROIC >20%), 10–14x for slow growers and cyclicals at mid-cycle.
   Never extrapolate a bubble multiple just because the stock "always
   trades rich."
3. **Margin of safety.** Discount the fair value by 10% for scores 80+,
   15% for 65–79, 20% for 50–64. Lower-conviction names need a bigger
   cushion.
4. Round to a clean number ($84, not $84.37). If the result is above
   the current price, the stock is already at value — mark it ✓ rather
   than inventing a higher target.

State the basis in the appendix (e.g., "buy-below $84 = 18x normalized
FCF/share of $5.50 − 15%"). This is a rubric-consistent value estimate,
not a price prediction — say so if the user asks.

## Calibration

- Steady fairly-priced blue chip ≈ 65–75. Reserve 85+ for exceptional.
- Pre-revenue companies: profitability, growth, and valuation pillars
  score near the floor by definition — a great story does not add points.
  Most land 20–35 regardless of hype.
- Cyclicals at peak earnings: score valuation on normalized earnings,
  not the peak-cycle P/E, and say so in the takeaway.
